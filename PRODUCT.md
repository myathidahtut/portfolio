# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Hiring managers, recruiters, and technical interviewers screening for Data Analyst /
Data Scientist / Developer roles in Berlin and the wider EU market. They spend
30-90 seconds on first pass: they're scanning for "is this a real 15-year engineer
who can also do data science, or a career-change portfolio padded with tutorial
projects." Secondary audience: engineers/peers who click through from GitHub or
LinkedIn to sanity-check the work before a referral.

## Product Purpose

A personal portfolio for Mya Thida Htut: 15 years shipping production software in
Myanmar (banking, POS/inventory, HR/payroll, hospitality, real estate, a
UNDP-funded SME marketplace), now transitioning into data science/AI after an MSc
from GISMA (Potsdam). The site's job is to make that transition read as credible
depth, not a reset to zero — real measured project metrics, a real multi-decade
timeline, real stack breadth. Success = a recruiter believes the seniority claim
within the first section and doesn't bounce.

## Brand Personality

Three words: **measured, evidentiary, unshowy.** Voice is a field log / ledger —
every claim is a dated, numbered entry with a result attached, not a marketing
adjective. Confidence comes from precision (exact percentages, exact dates, exact
DOIs) rather than from typographic loudness or self-congratulation.

## Anti-references

- Generic "AI-generated portfolio" template: hero-metric-card grid, gradient-text
  name, tiny uppercase tracked eyebrows over every section, numbered 01/02/03
  scaffolding used as decoration rather than an actual sequence, glassmorphism,
  bouncy card-hover-lift on everything.
- Bootcamp-grad portfolio voice: breathless framing of tutorial-tier projects as
  if they were flagship achievements, buzzword stacks with no shipped evidence.
- Anything that undersells or hides the 15-year engineering career in favor of a
  data-science-only narrative — the whole point is both halves are real.

## Design Principles

1. **Evidence over adjectives.** Every claim renders as a dated entry, a metric,
   or a linked artifact (repo, live demo, DOI) — never an unsupported superlative.
2. **The ledger identity is load-bearing, not decorative.** Numbered log entries,
   monospace data readouts, and the plate/timestamp motif exist because the
   content genuinely is a sequential record — preserve and extend this idiom
   rather than replacing it with generic card grids.
3. **Both careers get equal weight.** The 15-year engineering arc (banking,
   POS/inventory, HR, hospitality, real estate, UNDP marketplace) is not a
   footnote to the ML projects; it's the proof of seniority that makes the pivot
   credible.
4. **Quiet confidence, not volume.** Dark moss-green/brass, serif+sans+mono
   pairing, restrained motion. Nothing "shouts" — hierarchy carries weight, not
   size or saturation.
5. **No fabrication.** Every fact and metric must trace to the verified CV/resume
   source; if a figure isn't confirmed, it doesn't ship.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum: body text ≥4.5:1 contrast against its background at all times
(including any themed/dark surfaces), large text ≥3:1. Respect
`prefers-reduced-motion` for all reveal/scroll animations. Keyboard-navigable nav
and links with visible focus states. No information conveyed by color alone.
